Reconciling the comps vs. the AVMs — why they differ, and where we land

Two credible signals disagree, so we show both rather than picking the flattering one. The closed comps imply ~$470k: the 2025 Grand Landings new-builds (300/302/304 Grand Landings Pkwy) sold $482k–$580k and the 2023-built 499 Grand Landings Pkwy — same builder, same 2,498 sf as the subject — closed at $490,000, so on a like-for-like $/SF basis this home prices into the high-$400s. The four live AVMs imply ~$405k–$426k: automated models lean heavily on the home's own 2023 transaction (~$400k) and on the older, lower 2022 closings (484 Grand Landings $445k, 133 Wood Stork $480k), and they lag a fast-appreciating new-build pocket. Both are defensible; neither is the whole story.

Reconciled position: a value range of $440,000–$475,000 with a recommended list of $459,900 — deliberately set above every AVM (top AVM is Cotality at $425,967) and just under the comp-implied $470k, while staying below the active competition's $499,900–$529,900 asks. The one fact both methods agree on: the current $400,000 list is below market.

How the adjustments are calibrated

Why a feature is worth what it is worth here

A bathroom in a million-dollar home is worth far more than the same bathroom in a $50,000 home. Adjustments only make sense when scaled to the price point. Everything here uses the JHT $450k–$650k band, which is exactly where the Grand Landings comps sit.

Calibrated to this band ($450k–$650k)

  • Bedroom: $6,000 per bedroom of difference.
  • Full bath: $10,500; half bath: $3,500.

  • Private pool: $25,000 — used to hold out 145 S Coopers Hawk Way, not to adjust around.

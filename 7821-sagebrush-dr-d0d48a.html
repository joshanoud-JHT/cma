How the adjustments are calibrated

Why a feature is worth what it is worth here

A bathroom in a million-dollar home is worth far more than the same bathroom in a $50,000 home. Adjustments only make sense when they are scaled to the price point of the property. Everything in this analysis is calibrated to the $175k–$250k Port Richey market.

Calibrated to this market

  • Living area: about $95/sf for differences. That is below the $207 average sale price per foot, because the marginal value of extra space is always less than the average.
  • Private pool: roughly $15,000 of contributory value in this price band. In a luxury coastal home the same pool can add far more.
  • Bedroom / full bath: about $4,000 per bedroom and $7,500 per full bath in this tier, versus far more in a high-end home.

Why this matters for your list price

Buyers in this price band are payment-sensitive and shop on price per foot. Over-improving relative to the neighborhood rarely returns dollar-for-dollar, and under-pricing leaves money on the table. The adjustments above keep the comparison fair so the recommended price reflects what a buyer will actually pay for this specific home, not an average of dissimilar ones.

Methodology, confidence and sources

How this was built

Method

  • Subject facts pulled from the Pasco County Property Appraiser record and cross-checked against Zillow and RPR.
  • Comps drawn from Stellar MLS (closed, last 12 months) and held to the subject's profile: Regency Park single-family, 800–1,100 sq ft, no pool, built 1967–1987, lot under 0.25 acre. Private-pool homes were screened out; every comp sits in the same non-HOA, interior, non-canal subdivision.
  • Value derived three ways: the Stellar matched-set price per heated square foot, an adjusted sales-comparison grid on the closest neighborhood comps, and reconciliation against the AVMs.

Data confidence

  • High on subject facts (county-recorded) and all 23 matched comp sales (Stellar-verified closed sales).
  • The Realtor.com panel AVMs (RealEstimate, Cotality, Quantarium) are the agent-entered figures from the request; Collateral Analytics was not provided. The Realist RealAVM opened only through a Matrix SSO outside the automation, so it is shown as n/a; it is CoreLogic-derived and matches the Cotality figure.
  • The matched set was pulled directly from Stellar MLS, the primary MLS for Pasco County, filtered on subdivision, size, pool, and age. Private-pool homes and out-of-community homes were removed by reviewing each against the map.
  • All three MLS systems were searched with the same criteria, because agents outside the local board sometimes list a home in the "wrong" MLS. Stellar MLS is primary for Pasco County and is the basis here (23 matched Regency Park solds, 5 active, 2 pending). realMLS (Flexmls) was searched via its FBS data-share: postal code 34668 returned 2 out-of-area closed listings (an 1,838 sq ft home and a manufactured home), neither a Regency Park match. Citrus County MLS returned 16 listings in 34668, all in other Port Richey subdivisions, none in Regency Park. No comps were added outside Stellar.

How the adjustments are calibrated

Why a feature is worth what it is worth here

A bathroom in a million-dollar beach home is worth far more than the same bathroom in a $250,000 inland home. Adjustments only make sense scaled to the price point. Everything here is calibrated to the JHT $900K–$1.25M band.

Calibrated to this market

  • Bedroom: $8,000 per bedroom of difference. All three solds are 4-bed; the subject is a 3-bed with a larger owner's footprint.
  • Full bath: $13,500 (half bath $4,500). All three solds have 3 full baths vs. the subject's 2 full + 1 half.
  • Pool + cage: $35,000 contributory value in this band — applied to 1 Watchtower, the one sold without a pool.
  • Garage bay: $15,000. All three solds match the subject's 2-car garage, so no garage adjustments were needed.

Why this matters for your list price

Buyers cross-shopping Verona, Beachview Cove, and the ocean-view rows compare finished quality and total cost. The adjustments keep those comparisons honest, so the recommended price reflects what a buyer will actually pay for this specific home rather than an average of dissimilar ones. At this price point the model values are intentionally conservative; the live sliders let you stress-test them.

Methodology, confidence and sources

How this was built

Method

  • Subject facts pulled live from the Volusia County Property Appraiser (Land & Buildings record, fixture-bath count) and cross-checked against Realist, RPR, and Zillow.
  • Comps pulled live from all three MLS systems with identical criteria; combined and de-duplicated; oceanfront-tier and dissimilar-product records screened on the satellite map, not the MLS waterfront flag.
  • Value derived three ways: adjusted sold $/SF (price-band model), the bucket analysis (sold / pending / active), and reconciliation against the seven-source AVM panel.

Data confidence

  • High on subject facts (county record, pulled this session) and on all three sold comps (MLS close records; 1 Watchtower also county-verified via tax roll).
  • Medium on the 24-month sold window: only 3 matched sales exist, and 62 Watchtower closed 23 months ago (no time adjustment applied; conservative).
  • Medium on 170 Coquina Key (second-row ocean view; location premium runs in the comp's favor and is noted rather than separately quantified).
  • Collateral Analytics AVM was not provided on the request and is shown as unavailable; the other six AVM sources are present.
  • Beachview Cove pins on the comp map are street-level approximations (new plat not yet in the geocoder); all other pins are parcel-point geocodes.

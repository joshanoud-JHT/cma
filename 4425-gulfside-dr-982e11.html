The adjustment model

How each sale was adjusted

Adjustments scale to the price point of the property. A bathroom in a $900,000 home is worth more than the same bathroom in a $200,000 home, so this analysis uses the $600K to $1M band values throughout. Beds, baths, garage and pool are adjusted numerically. Water frontage, dock, lift, condition and year are handled through comparable selection and the reconciliation below rather than a fixed dollar figure, because on waterfront those items vary too much to price with a single number.

Methodology, confidence and sources

How this was built

  • Subject facts pulled from the Pasco County Property Appraiser (parcel 06-26-16-001B-00000-1390) and the home's prior MLS listing. County heated area (1,813 sq ft) is used, not Zillow square footage.
  • Comparable sales searched in all three MLS systems: Stellar MLS (the controlling system for Pasco, 103 closed matches in the size and water band, plus 47 active and pending), realMLS/Flexmls (0 unique; its data-share mirrors Stellar), and Citrus County MLS (49 records in the zip, all either duplicates of Stellar or inland homes not comparable to a Gulf-access waterfront home). No unique comparables were added by the other two.
  • Value derived water-body first per the team's waterfront method: same water body and frontage type first, a 24-month closed window, and two reconciled approaches (direct comparables and land-plus-building extraction), anchored on the home's own last sale.
  • Seven AVMs reviewed (Zillow, Realtor.com RealEstimate, Collateral Analytics, Cotality/CoreLogic, Quantarium; RPR and Realist not retrieved this session). AVMs are shown for context and not weighted, because they cannot price waterfront attributes.
  • Confidence: 7 of 10. The same-community comparable set is strong and the anchors agree. Residual uncertainty comes from variation in individual canal position and frontage among comparables, and from a thin, slow luxury waterfront segment.

How the adjustments are calibrated

Why a feature is worth what it is worth here

A bathroom in a million-dollar home is worth far more than the same bathroom in a $50,000 home. Adjustments only make sense when they are scaled to the price point of the property. Everything in this analysis uses the JHT values for the $100K–$300K band.

Calibrated to this market

  • Bedroom: $4,000. Full bath: $7,500. Half bath: $2,500.
  • Garage bay: $10,000 — deliberately heavy in this band, because a real 2-bay garage is scarce in compact townhomes and buyers pay for it (it is what separates this home from 2 Park Place and 210 Timberline).
  • Private pool: $15,000 — unused here; neither the subject nor any kept comp has one.

Why this matters for your list price

Buyers in this band are payment-sensitive and shop on price per foot — but they shop within their size class. Compact 2/2 townhomes clear at $204–$239/SF while 1,400+ sq ft units clear at $159–$172/SF. Pricing this 1,132 sq ft end unit off the blended average would give away the size premium; pricing it off the dissimilar large units' $/SF would overshoot. The grid keeps the comparison honest in both directions.

Citrus County MLS (Matrix): City = Ormond Beach, A+P+Closed-365d = 0 matches (searched, zero). De-duplicated by address/parcel across systems (101 Cotton Seed, 195 Deer Lake, 4 Misners, 210 Lemon Tree, 30 Landings, 56 Park Pl appeared twice; fullest record kept).
  • 365-day sold window: only three matched solds closed inside 180 days — fewer than the five needed for reliable statistics — so the window was widened to a full year and that fact is stated wherever the stats appear.
  • Map screening: every comp was reviewed on the map, not just MLS flags. This is interior Ormond Beach — no saltwater-canal issue exists in this market segment and no canal or waterfront comps were kept; the one lakefront sale (187 Deer Lake Cir, $310,000) was excluded for that reason.
